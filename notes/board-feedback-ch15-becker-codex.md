**Strengths**  
Chapter 15 is strongest where it does sociology in the concrete sense: showing control mechanisms in ordinary institutional scenes. The opening red-pen vignette makes “social stabilization” observable rather than abstract (`chapters/chapter15.tex:4`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:6`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:14`). The mixed-bin metaphor is also method-friendly: it translates a conceptual claim into a measurement problem researchers already understand (`chapters/chapter15.tex:54`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:64`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:73`). You then give usable definitions of the three conditioning variables, not just labels: dialect, register, discourse community (`chapters/chapter15.tex:91`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:108`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:127`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:131`). The timescale notes are especially useful for empirical design (`chapters/chapter15.tex:103`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:122`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:133`). This all sets up the Chapter 16 gauntlet well (`chapters/chapter16.tex:96`, `chapters/chapter16.tex:99`).

**Major Concerns**  
The main problem is usability for working researchers: the chapter tells us what is true but not yet what to do Monday morning. The formal object is named (`chapters/chapter15.tex:73`), but there is no explicit coding protocol, no minimum-data workflow, and no criterion for when conditioning has actually restored projectibility. That is a missed opportunity because the planning doc asked for diagnostics in exactly this spot (`notes/chapter15_planning.md:28`, `notes/chapter15_planning.md:29`), and the Becker board note asks for claims testable with ordinary field constraints (`notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:154`, `notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:157`).  
Second, the disciplinary rhetoric becomes needlessly oppositional in places (“they were wrong,” “realism proper”), which risks tribal resistance (`chapters/chapter15.tex:194`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:206`). You can keep high standards without sounding like you are invalidating prior work.  
Third, Chapter 14 already models operational discipline with explicit two-part tests (`chapters/chapter14.tex:217`), and Chapter 15 should match that standard for internal coherence.
