**Strengths**
- Chapter 15 successfully reframes variation as a conditioning problem rather than decay. The mixed-bin argument and the explicit \(P(\text{Form}\mid D,R,C)\) formulation are the chapter’s strongest conceptual move, and they operationalize “social stabilization” better than abstract claims alone (`chapters/chapter15.tex:73`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:154`).
- The three-mechanism structure (dialect, register, discourse community) is clear and usefully time-scaled through concrete loops, which gives the chapter explanatory texture instead of mere taxonomy (`chapters/chapter15.tex:103`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:122`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:133`).
- The Nunberg-to-Hacking passage is the best historical-ontological moment: it shows classification as intervention, not passive description. The “slur” case is explicitly historical and genuinely interactive (`chapters/chapter15.tex:139`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:141`).

**Major Concerns**
- The chapter does not yet cleanly separate natural-kind-style stabilization from looping human-kind stabilization. Chapter 14’s detector/homeostasis story can be read as non-interactive maintenance (`chapters/chapter14.tex:39`), while Chapter 15 uses the same “homeostasis” vocabulary for label-mediated feedback (`chapters/chapter15.tex:143`) without stating a criterion that distinguishes the two kinds of stabilization.
- Label modeling is uneven. “Standard” appears as a condition and policing regime (`chapters/chapter15.tex:65`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:114`), and “slur” is historical (`chapters/chapter15.tex:139`), but “native speaker” is absent in Chapter 15 despite being flagged as central in planning (`notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:56`, `notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:98`). That weakens the looping claim.
- The looping claim is asserted as constant (“the loop is constant”) but not tied to a defeat condition (`chapters/chapter15.tex:143`). Chapter 16 has falsification machinery (`chapters/chapter16.tex:73`, `chapters/chapter16.tex:124`), but Chapter 15 does not map its label claims onto that machinery.

**Priority Fixes**
- Add a short explicit contrast box in Chapter 15: “non-interactive stabilizers” vs “interactive (looping) stabilizers,” with one diagnostic for each. Anchor the first to Chapter 14’s detector logic (`chapters/chapter14.tex:60`) and the second to label uptake dynamics (`chapters/chapter15.tex:141`).
- Insert a dedicated “native speaker as interactive kind” subsection near the current looping passage (`chapters/chapter15.tex:141`). Treat it as a feedback mechanism: classification -> gatekeeping response -> behavioral adaptation -> distributional shift. This directly satisfies the board’s unresolved question (`notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:56`).
- Make looping falsifiable by adding preregisterable predictions in Chapter 15 that feed Chapter 16’s gauntlet: if labels are causal, labeled contexts should add predictive lift over unlabeled baselines; if not, looping is not supported (`chapters/chapter16.tex:118`, `chapters/chapter16.tex:124`).
