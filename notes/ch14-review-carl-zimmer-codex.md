**Strengths**
- The chapter has a strong organizing claim and repeats it with discipline: grammaticality as a maintained form-value coupling, with acceptability as detector output. Sections “The HPC claim” and “The stabilizer: a feeling” do real conceptual work by separating structural meaning from truth-conditional semantics and from pragmatic felicity.
- The illusion material is well chosen and rhetorically effective. Pairing “The horse raced past the barn fell” with the comparative illusion makes your “detector, not oracle” thesis concrete, not merely sloganized.
- The prose often lands for non-specialists: “wiring diagram,” “zipper,” and “immune response” can carry readers through abstract terrain. The chapter also earns points for trying to produce falsifiers (“Definiteness thread”).

**Major Concerns**
- The mechanism is still under-specified. You often name components (“Bayesian,” “entrenchment,” “processing ease”) but do not define computationally what is being updated, what counts as an error signal, or how thresholds are set. As written, “zipper/immune system/spinning top” risks reading as metaphor stack rather than model.
- In “Entrenchment in action,” the account threatens circularity: detector tracks entrenched coupling, and coupling is what gets entrenched. A skeptic will ask where independent traction comes from. Without external criteria, this can look like relabeling acceptability patterns as ontology.
- “The phenomenology of coupling: What it’s like” is partly disciplined, but the Peircean mapping (“sign/object/interpretant,” “would-be”) may lose an intelligent non-specialist right when evidential demands should tighten. The section says phenomenology is “not explanatory load-bearing,” then leans on introspective typology as if architecture-confirming evidence.
- Risky claims are unevenly calibrated. Some are productively falsifiable; others feel over-precise placeholder-like (“~3-sigma absence,” specific causal story for unsatiatable LBE) without enough method detail for a skeptical linguist to trust the precision.
- Acceptability vs grammaticality is not fully stabilized. You draw the distinction clearly, then blur it with “the reality is constituted by what gets entrenched.” Philosophers will press: is grammaticality detector-independent or not? Right now the chapter oscillates.

**Priority Fixes**
- Add one explicit mechanistic box in “The stabilizer: a feeling”: inputs, latent representation, update rule, and output (judgment). Even light formalization (state variables and arrows) would shift this from evocative to testable.
- Tighten the acceptability/grammaticality relation with a clear stance: either inferential access to an independent structure, or community-constituted structure with explicit consequences for disagreement and change.
- Rework “Three registers” for accessibility: first plain-language definitions and one worked example, then triadic mapping as a secondary gloss.
- Trim or qualify strongest causal claims in “Satiation” unless backed by methods/results in chapter-local detail. Skeptical readers punish precision without visible evidential scaffolding.
- Soften “syntax owns it” in “Value at every grain.” Interface phenomena make exclusive ownership sound overstated and theoretically provincial.

**Decisive Tests**
1. Longitudinal exposure study on marginal constructions with preregistered predictions: which items should satiate, which should resist, and by how much across sessions.
2. Cross-community calibration test (dialect/register groups) using identical stimuli, separating confidence, reaction time, and repair behavior. This directly probes your “community-indexed detector” claim.
3. Dissociation experiment: manipulate processing difficulty independently of grammatical well-formedness (e.g., garden-path load vs matched controls) to quantify false positives and false negatives in the detector.
4. Entrenchment-shift intervention for a novel sequence (your determiner-thread prediction): track cohort-structured adoption and lag in formal norms. If uptake occurs without judgment shift, your maintenance account takes a direct hit.
