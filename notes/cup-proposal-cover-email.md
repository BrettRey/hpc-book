Dear [Editor Name],

I am writing to submit a book proposal for *Words That Won't Hold Still* for consideration in Cambridge Studies in Linguistics.

The manuscript is complete at approximately 494 pages and 18 chapters. It argues that many linguistic categories are real kinds without essences and develops that claim across countability, definiteness, lexical categories, gender, sign language anaphora, phonological contrast, constructions, register, grammaticality, and social stabilization.

I am a co-author, with Rodney Huddleston and Geoffrey K. Pullum, of *A Student's Introduction to English Grammar* (2nd ed., Cambridge University Press, 2022). I also have an ESL textbook, *Language Landscapes: The ESL Teacher's Guide to How English Works*, accepted by Language Science Press, and my recent work has appeared in venues including *Journal of Linguistics*, *English Language and Linguistics*, and *Dictionaries*.

I believe the project is a strong fit for Cambridge Studies in Linguistics because it addresses a foundational question with implications across general, theoretical, and descriptive linguistics: what linguistic categories are, and what makes them projectible enough to support explanation.
