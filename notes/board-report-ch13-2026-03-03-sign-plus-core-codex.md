# Advisory Board Report: Chapter 13 (Sign-language anaphor)

**Date**: 2026-03-03  
**Scope**: `chapters/chapter13-sign-anaphor.tex` (+ ch13 sign-lit notes)  
**Method**: 10 independent Codex subagent reviews run in parallel  
**Board mix**: full-range core board + added sign-language specialists

## Convergent Criticisms (high confidence)

### 1) Teleological overstatement in emergence/coupling claims
**Convergence**: Sandler, Lillo-Martin, Perniss, Tufte, McCloskey, Hacking, Goldberg, Meier  
**Locations**: `chapter13-sign-anaphor.tex:162`, `:186`  
**Issue**: Wording implies a universal developmental law (tracking first, agreement later as languages age), while evidence in your own notes is ecology-sensitive and non-linear.

### 2) Schlenker/Kuhn bridge is under-argued and too binary
**Convergence**: Goldberg, Hacking, Lillo-Martin, Zimmer, McCloskey  
**Location**: `chapter13-sign-anaphor.tex:90`  
**Issue**: “Both accounts are right” / theorist-to-cluster mapping reads as asserted. Needs one more inferential bridge and explicit falsifiable prediction.

### 3) Verdict overreach (especially projection and null-argument licensing)
**Convergence**: Tufte, Lillo-Martin, Sandler, Meier, McCloskey  
**Location**: `chapter13-sign-anaphor.tex:200`  
**Issue**: Claims are too categorical relative to mixed evidence; should be probabilistic and scoped.

### 4) Sign-specific terminology needs precision
**Convergence**: Meier, Cormier, Sandler, Lillo-Martin  
**Locations**: `chapter13-sign-anaphor.tex:40`, `:42`, `:77`  
**Issue**: Current phrasing can imply cleaner pronoun/gesture and person/agreement paradigms than supported (hybrid indexicality; first/non-first salience; analysis debated).

### 5) Modality claims need hedging and gesture comparator
**Convergence**: Perniss, McCloskey, Tufte (partial), Sandler (partial)  
**Locations**: `chapter13-sign-anaphor.tex:24`, `:104`  
**Issue**: Some statements read as universals; evidence supports strong tendencies in studied systems, plus domain-specific signed-vs-gesture contrasts rather than absolute sign/gesture splits.

## Secondary Convergences

### 6) Evidence display in tables is less auditable than prose evidence
**Convergence**: Tufte, Hacking  
**Locations**: `chapter13-sign-anaphor.tex:68-88`, `:166-184`, `:196-206`  
**Issue**: Table 2 uses checkmarks/labels without thresholds/denominators; verdict section has broad claims with little direct citation anchoring.

### 7) Humour/register is mostly working, but a few lines dilute key inferential moments
**Convergence**: McCloskey (main), Zimmer (partial)  
**Locations**: especially `chapter13-sign-anaphor.tex:58`, `:130`, `:200`  
**Issue**: Keep wit, but trim at the precise points where evidential force is being claimed.

## Divergences / Open Choices
- **Role shift as backup vs co-primary**: Goldberg/Sandler press for “co-primary” framing in maintenance contexts; current “backup” framing may underplay narrative data.
- **How far to hedge agreement claims**: Meier/Sandler want stronger caution on agreement status terminology; could be done lightly in one sentence rather than restructuring section.
- **Humour intensity**: no one argues for dry prose, but recommendation is to concentrate humour in setup/examples, not verdict-level claims.

## Priority Edit Set (ranked)

1. **De-teleologize emergence/coupling lines** (`:162`, `:186`)  
   Replace universal trajectory language with ecology-/history-sensitive wording.

2. **Rewrite the Schlenker/Kuhn bridge** (`:90`)  
   Keep dual-cluster interpretation, but phrase as overlapping emphases plus a falsifiable prediction.

3. **Scope the projection claim in verdict** (`:200`)  
   Change from categorical to probabilistic predictions; narrow null-argument claim.

4. **Tighten sign-specific terminology** (`:40`, `:42`, `:77`)  
   Use hybrid indexical/pronominal wording; avoid implying settled 1/2/3 agreement paradigm.

5. **Hedge modality and gesture contrast** (`:24`, `:104`)  
   “Comparable in studied systems” and “domain-specific contrast” wording.

6. **Make Table 2 auditable (optional but high value)** (`:166-184`)  
   Add denominators/uncertainty or convert to evidence table format.

## Net Assessment
The chapter’s core architecture is strong and widely endorsed: the two-cluster decomposition (tracking vs agreement), mechanism/failure-mode framing, and cross-linguistic decoupling argument all survived independent scrutiny. The main fixes are precision and calibration, not structural redesign.
