# Chapter 8: Failure Modes

Chapter 8 is the gatekeeping chapter of Part II: it takes the HPC framework built across Chapters 4 through 7 (kinds, discreteness, projectibility, stabilizers) and asks what it looks like when the framework *doesn't* apply. The central claim is that a putative category must pass two independent diagnostics to qualify as an HPC kind: projectibility (can you predict new data from old?) and homeostasis (can you name the stabilizing mechanisms?). Failure on either diagnostic is informative, and the chapter taxonomizes three characteristic failure modes: thin clustering, fat clustering, and negative diagnosis.

The chapter opens by naming the *inflation problem*: if HPC criteria are too loose, the framework explains nothing because it excludes nothing. This is not a philosopher's worry but a practical one, reinforced by institutional scaffolding (curricula, tagsets, exams) that can sustain labels long after their mechanistic warrant has expired. Khalidi's notion of *epistemic kinds* is invoked to distinguish categories that serve our purposes from categories that track causal structure.

Each failure mode gets a dedicated case study. For thin clustering, preposition copying and pruning in English (documented by Radford and Felser from broadcast speech) serves as the "smoke ring" example: a real pattern with vanishing frequency, no social indexing, and no functional niche, generated as incidental fallout from the competition between pied-piping and stranding. Miller's nonce-word test for *cromulent* gives a second illustration of mechanistic thinness. For fat clustering, the traditional category *adverb* is the paradigmatic wastebasket, lumping manner adverbs, degree words, and sentence adverbs whose mechanisms, syntactic distributions, and acquisition paths are radically different. Ernst's ordering hierarchy provides the distributional evidence. The extended treatment of Huddleston's puzzle over *otherwise* is a demonstration case, showing how a wastebasket category groans when forced to behave like a kind. For negative diagnosis, *non-finite clause* is the case study: four clause types (infinitival, plain-form, past-participial, gerund-participial) grouped only by the absence of primary tense inflection, with no shared positive cluster and no shared stabilizers. The Left Branch Condition provides a supplementary case: a "gap of abstinence" maintained not by a dedicated constraint but by the sheer density of competitor strategies.

The chapter then introduces the *grain question* (the Madagascar fallacy: an ecosystem of kinds is not itself a kind) and a section on lexical semantics explaining why semantic categories feel slippery when conceptual similarity, inferential profile, and grammatical patterning cross-cut. A formal HPC-kind audit template (seven steps, from target scoping through falsifier specification) is presented as a reusable instrument for Part III.

The chapter closes with a coupling coda that distinguishes hard coupling (phonemes: form determines meaning with minimal slippage), loose coupling (grammatical categories: form and meaning drift independently), and composite coupling (constructions: conventionalized form-meaning pairings stabilized by use). This continuum predicts how categories behave under perturbation and sets up the case studies in Part III, where countability, definiteness, lexical categories, and gender each occupy a different position along it.

The argument is well-structured and the case studies are concrete. One tension worth noting: the projectibility diagnostic is operationalized as a train-on-one-subclass-test-on-another procedure, but no actual quantitative results are reported for the adverb or non-finite clause cases (Polish aspect in Chapter 7 is the only worked numerical example). The claim that *adverb* fails the diagnostic rests on distributional argumentation from Ernst rather than on a literal held-out test. This is persuasive but leaves the diagnostic looking more like a heuristic than the falsifiable procedure the chapter advertises. A second mild tension: the coupling coda is substantial enough to feel like a distinct contribution rather than a section of a chapter on failure modes, and the reader may wonder whether it belongs here or in a chapter of its own.
