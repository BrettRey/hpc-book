## Consensus Weaknesses (prioritised)

### 1. Three-register phenomenology / Peirce apparatus
**Flagged by:** Lesser, Becker, Morris (3/6)

Becker: "dead weight." Lesser: "prime real estate for speculative architecture." Morris: "a guest lecture, not a character."

**Diagnosis:** The triadic mapping (qualitative jolt / reactive resistance / normative correction → emotional / energetic / logical interpretants) is speculative architecture with no concrete scene showing the dissociation in action. Table is predictions, not findings.

**Options:**
- A: Add a minimal triple — one sentence that triggers each register differently (or two of three) — so the reader *experiences* the dissociation.
- B: Compress to a paragraph + table and demote from mid-chapter real estate.
- C: Cut the Peircean labels entirely; keep the three registers as phenomenological observation without the semiotic mapping.

**Note:** The Peirce apparatus is load-bearing for the book's larger architecture (Ch 7, Ch 13, Ch 14). Cutting it here has downstream costs. Option A is probably right: the framework stays, but it needs flesh.

### 2. Dual-role / HPC audit too compressed
**Flagged by:** Becker, Zimmer (2/6)

Becker: "conceptual hinge of the book" gets two paragraphs. Zimmer: HPC audit "compressed into a paragraph" — wants the cluster structure shown with worked examples of partial independence.

**Options:**
- Expand the audit to show partial independence concretely (the *whose* / *finished it yesterday* examples are there but rushed).
- Show the enforcement-mechanism loop in action: how nounhood is maintained *by* grammaticality specifically.

### 3. Homer coda needs a real case
**Flagged by:** Becker, Morris (2/6)

Becker: "undermines your own argument" — one celebrity isn't how social HPCs shift. Morris: imaginary when a real case would land harder.

**Options:**
- Replace Homer with a real norm-shift (singular *they*, *literally* as intensifier, or split infinitive).
- Keep Homer as the thought experiment but add a real case alongside it to show the mechanism has actually operated.
- Develop the perturbation dynamics: what does it take to shift community entrenchment? Critical mass, not one speaker.

### 4. Mechanism sketch needs concrete analogue
**Flagged by:** Lesser, Zimmer (2/6)

Lesser: "a flowchart forced into prose." Zimmer: "incantation" — labels, not moving parts.

**Counterpoint:** Tufte says "exemplary — exactly what a displayed element should do." The disagreement is audience: information-design vs. narrative-momentum.

**Options:**
- Add a one-sentence physical analogue (thermostat, voltage regulator) to anchor the five parts.
- Compress to three sentences and move the diagnostic consequence to the front.
- Leave it — Tufte's right that it compresses well; the problem may be the paragraphs flanking it, not the box itself.

The 2×2 prediction (monolingual/bilingual × LBE/age construction) is buried in prose. A small table with predicted directions would make the testable contrast visible.
