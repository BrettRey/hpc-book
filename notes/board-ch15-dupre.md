**1. One-Sentence Summary**  
The chapter argues that grammaticality is neither an essence nor merely a judgment, but a socially maintained homeostatic coupling between morphosyntactic form and structural meaning, while acceptability is the noisy output of a fallible detector of that coupling (lines 7-9, 19-23, 61-67).

**2. Strengths**
- The central distinction between grammaticality and acceptability is unusually clear and productive: lines 61-79 separate coupling, processing, detector output, threshold, and entrenchment update in a way that makes the ontology empirically tractable rather than merely metaphorical.
- The chapter is strongest where it resists essentialism without collapsing into nominalism. Lines 241-253 and 352-358 give a genuinely pluralist picture of thinning, divergence, contact, and contingency while still insisting that these are structured, projectible realities rather than arbitrary labels.
- You do not merely redescribe the phenomenon; you try to expose it to risk. The operationalization in lines 134-152 and the falsifiability language in lines 333-346 are exactly what this kind of philosophical intervention needs.

**3. Weaknesses**
- I see why you want both local plurality and a higher-order account of “grammaticality itself,” but the chapter has not yet earned that transition. Lines 15-21 present grammaticality as one form-value pairing among many, whereas lines 256-268 and 344-346 treat it as a system-level HPC with fairly unified status. You need explicit individuation criteria: when are there many legitimate grammaticalities, and when is there one higher-order kind with local variants?
- The detector story is suggestive, but at present it risks circularity. Lines 61-79 and 86-88 say the maintained coupling tunes the detector and the detector, over time, tunes the coupling. “Reflexive equilibrium” is a fair description, but not yet a sufficient mechanism. The revision should say what evidence would show that the felt ungrammaticality itself, rather than correction practices, prestige effects, or distributional learning more generally, does indispensable stabilizing work.
- The evidential register needs tightening. Lines 152 and 274-275 properly distinguish prediction from evidence, but line 262 relies on “impressionistic reports,” and lines 279-281 and 344-345 lean on empirical findings that need verification. More importantly, the chapter should say more sharply what the HPC account explains beyond a familiar competence/performance split plus gradient processing effects; otherwise some of the contrast with “essentialism” at line 28 is too easy.

**4. Key Question**  
What are the identity conditions for the kind you call “grammaticality” once discourse communities are overlapping, nested, and shifting (lines 230-234, 323-324): when do we have one kind with variants, and when do we have several equally real kinds?

**5. Verdict**  
Revise & Resubmit: philosophically ambitious and often compelling, but it needs a cleaner account of kind individuation and stricter separation of mechanism, evidence, and metaphor.
