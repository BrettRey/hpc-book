**Remaining Concerns**
- The mechanism is explicit, but still under-identified. “Coupling state” and “detector output” are latent constructs without clear measurement rules. A reviewer can still say this is architecture by metaphor rather than a model with bridge principles.
- The metaphor stack is now overloaded: zipper, immune system, spinning top, basin, standing wave, phase transition. A non-specialist will lose the through-line and wonder which metaphor is literal enough to generate predictions.
- `Value at every grain` and `Grammaticality as a species of appropriateness` risk scope drift. You start with a precise claim (morphosyntax ↔ structural meaning) and then broaden into a general appropriateness ecology. Philosophers may read this as moving the target.
- A few assertions still sound too hard-edged for current evidence: e.g., “left-branch extraction… persists across tasks, speakers, and presentation modes,” and the definiteness thread’s “final filter… grammatical requirement.” These need tighter scoping by variety, register, and dataset.
- Ownership/boundary language is mostly improved (“syntax gets first pass… not sole authority”), but some passages still imply jurisdictional partitioning across subfields as if settled, rather than field-relative analytic convenience.

**Decisive Tests (what now most matters)**
- Orthogonal dissociation experiment: structural mismatch (yes/no) × processing load (high/low). If detector theory is right, mismatch and load should show separable effects across ratings, repairs, and latency.
- Triadic factor test: do “jolt,” “resistance,” and “normative correction” form three partly independent latent factors, or collapse into one disfluency factor?
- Longitudinal entrenchment test: repeated exposure plus social-index manipulation (cohort/register cues) should shift thresholds in predicted order; pure processing accounts should not match that pattern.
- Out-of-sample prediction benchmark: can a model using entrenchment history + processing metrics predict acceptability/repair better than frequency/surprisal alone? That is the key adjudication point now.
