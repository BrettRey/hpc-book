**Strengths**
This is a substantial improvement. The mechanism is now explicit enough to be discussable as a model, not just a metaphor: the five-part loop in “The stabilizer: a feeling” (coupling state, processing channel, detector output, threshold, entrenchment update) and the explicit timescale separation are exactly what skeptical readers need. The triadic phenomenology is also now operationalized, not merely named: “Three registers” plus Table 14.1 gives observables, confounds, and falsifiers. The chapter also handles circularity better than before: the line “reflexive equilibrium dynamic, not a vicious circle” is not cosmetic; it is backed by a time-slice vs diachronic distinction. Finally, “Grammaticality as a species of appropriateness” is a strong boundary move that softens syntax-imperial claims while preserving analytic traction.

**Remaining Concerns**
The main residual risk is comparative-concept drift into English-descriptive reification. Several claims still read as if they are near-universal properties of “grammar” rather than language-particular profiles: e.g., “The progressive … is required for the ongoing-action construal,” “Left-branch extraction remains categorically rejected regardless of exposure,” and “This table explains why grammaticality is primarily diagnosed at the morphosyntactic grain.” Those are defensible as English-centered claims; they are not yet disciplined as cross-linguistic comparative claims.

Relatedly, “Value at every grain” risks reifying one architecture (phoneme > morpheme > word > construction > clause type) as typological baseline. Radical constructionist and typological readers will ask whether this is a comparative heuristic or an ontological commitment. That distinction should be explicit.

On mechanism specification: better, but still partly verbal. You name components, but you do not yet specify independent measurement strategy for each variable. Without that, critics will say coupling strength and detector threshold are both inferred from the same acceptability behavior, and the old circularity re-enters methodologically.

Boundary/ownership language is improved but not fully neutralized. “Syntax gets first pass on it” is better than exclusivism, but it can still sound jurisdictional in a book otherwise committed to field-relative projectibility.

**Priority Fixes**
1. Add a short “comparative concept discipline” paragraph (likely in “What doesn’t count” or “HPC audit”) explicitly distinguishing language-specific descriptive categories from cross-linguistic comparative concepts.  
2. Recalibrate absolute statements into scoped claims (“in present-day mainstream English corpora/judgments…”).  
3. Add one paragraph specifying how each mechanism variable is measured independently (corpus distribution, processing load metrics, online mismatch signal, repair behavior, longitudinal update).  
4. Replace one English-only anchor example with a typologically different system to reduce Anglo reification risk.

**Decisive Tests (what now most matters)**
1. A factorial experiment manipulating structural mismatch and processing load independently, with separate readouts for jolt, resistance, and normative correction (testing Table 14.1 dissociability).  
2. A longitudinal entrenchment study on an emerging construction (register-stratified), testing whether detector thresholds and repair norms shift in the predicted order.  
3. A cross-linguistic matched-construal design (e.g., age/aspect/argument-structure domains) to test whether “same intended value, different entrenched form” predicts different mismatch profiles.  
4. Direct model comparison with relevance-based accounts (“Relevance and the immune system”) using preregistered discriminating predictions.

This chapter is now close to publishable; the remaining work is mostly calibration and comparative rigor.
