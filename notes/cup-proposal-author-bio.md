## Short author bio

Brett Reynolds is a linguist at Humber Polytechnic and the University of Toronto whose work brings philosophy of science into contact with grammatical theory, especially in the study of grammatical categories, grammaticality, and linguistic kinds. He is a co-author, with Rodney Huddleston and Geoffrey K. Pullum, of *A Student's Introduction to English Grammar* (2nd ed., Cambridge University Press, 2022), author of the forthcoming ESL textbook *Language Landscapes: The ESL Teacher's Guide to How English Works* (Language Science Press), and co-editor, with Peter Evans, of Otto Jespersen's *Negation in English and Other Languages* (Language Science Press, 2025). His recent articles have appeared in *Journal of Linguistics*, *English Language and Linguistics*, *Dictionaries*, and *Cadernos de Linguística*.

## Slightly fuller version

Brett Reynolds is a linguist at Humber Polytechnic and the University of Toronto whose research brings philosophy of science into contact with grammatical theory. His work focuses on the ontological status of linguistic categories, grammaticality, and the mechanisms that stabilize category structure across speakers and over time. He is a co-author, with Rodney Huddleston and Geoffrey K. Pullum, of *A Student's Introduction to English Grammar* (2nd ed., Cambridge University Press, 2022), author of the forthcoming ESL textbook *Language Landscapes: The ESL Teacher's Guide to How English Works* (Language Science Press), and co-editor, with Peter Evans, of Otto Jespersen's *Negation in English and Other Languages* (Language Science Press, 2025). His articles have appeared in *Journal of Linguistics*, *English Language and Linguistics*, *Dictionaries*, and *Cadernos de Linguística*, alongside other work on English syntax, lexical categories, countability, and grammar pedagogy.
