**One-sentence summary**

The chapter argues that grammaticality is not a timeless property of sentences but a socially maintained, homeostatic coupling between morphosyntactic form and structural meaning, while acceptability judgments are only a noisy, community-calibrated detector of that coupling ([chapter15.tex#L7](/Users/brettreynolds/Documents/LLM-CLI-projects/papers/HPC%20book/chapters/chapter15.tex#L7); lines 7-9, 61-69, 352-358).

**Strengths**

- The chapter’s central conceptual distinction is strong: separating `licensing` from `coupling`, and then separating `grammaticality` from `acceptability`, gives the argument real analytical traction instead of merely redescribing intuitions ([chapter15.tex#L21](/Users/brettreynolds/Documents/LLM-CLI-projects/papers/HPC%20book/chapters/chapter15.tex#L21); lines 21-23 and 61-69). This is the point at which the book most clearly moves from metaphysics to mechanism.

- The mechanism sketch is unusually good. By distinguishing token-time processing, individual entrenchment, and community norm dynamics, the chapter actually proposes a micro-to-macro story rather than invoking “usage” as a black box ([chapter15.tex#L69](/Users/brettreynolds/Documents/LLM-CLI-projects/papers/HPC%20book/chapters/chapter15.tex#L69); lines 69-85). The identification strategy in lines 82-85 is especially valuable because it tells the reader what evidence would discriminate competing explanations.

- The breakdown section is persuasive because it treats failure as structured rather than accidental: thinning, divergence, and contact interference are exactly the kinds of differentiated outcomes one expects from a socially maintained norm system ([chapter15.tex#L240](/Users/brettreynolds/Documents/LLM-CLI-projects/papers/HPC%20book/chapters/chapter15.tex#L240); lines 240-253). That is the chapter at its most convincing from a social-ontology perspective.

**Weaknesses**

- The chapter has the right target, but the collective-action mechanism is still underdescribed. You say repair reshapes coupling and that speakers switch communities dynamically, yet you never quite explain how innumerable local corrections aggregate into a stable equilibrium rather than a patchwork of idiolects ([chapter15.tex#L69](/Users/brettreynolds/Documents/LLM-CLI-projects/papers/HPC%20book/chapters/chapter15.tex#L69); lines 69-80, 230-234, 323-324). Actionable fix: add one short subsection on the social mechanism proper, specifying the roles of coordination benefits, prestige asymmetries, uptake, and differential sanctioning.

- The “dual role” claim risks explanatory circularity. The chapter does acknowledge the problem, but as written grammaticality sometimes appears to be both the maintained state and the thing doing the maintaining, which blurs causal levels ([chapter15.tex#L86](/Users/brettreynolds/Documents/LLM-CLI-projects/papers/HPC%20book/chapters/chapter15.tex#L86); lines 86-88 and 264-268). Actionable fix: state explicitly what the independent variables are at each level: detector output, repair practice, entrenchment history, and coupling state should not be allowed to slide into one another rhetorically.

- The empirical sections occasionally overstate what has been shown. To your credit, line 152 marks one table as predictive, but elsewhere the prose moves too quickly from “plausible support” to “audit passed,” especially in the satiation and neural-evidence discussion ([chapter15.tex#L274](/Users/brettreynolds/Documents/LLM-CLI-projects/papers/HPC%20book/chapters/chapter15.tex#L274); lines 274-281 and 344-346). The specific findings attributed to `lufrankdegen2024`, `Nevins2007`, `Zawiszewski2009`, and `MuralikrishnanIdrissi2021` need verification, since I have not read those sources. Actionable fix: mark more sharply which claims are established, which are suggestive, and which are straightforwardly promissory.

**Key question**

If grammaticality is a decentralized social equilibrium rather than a rule enforced from above, what exactly is the mechanism by which local repair behavior produces community-wide convergence, especially when speakers move across overlapping communities and prestige hierarchies pull in different directions?

**Verdict**

**Revise & Resubmit**: the chapter has a strong conceptual core and a promising mechanism sketch, but it needs a sharper micro-to-macro social mechanism and stricter evidential discipline before the main claim is fully convincing.
