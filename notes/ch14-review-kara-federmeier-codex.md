Strengths
- The chapter has a clear, ambitious thesis and states it early: grammaticality as a maintained form–value coupling (`§The HPC claim`, “zipper” language). That is conceptually stronger than treating grammaticality as a primitive yes/no feature.
- `§The phenomenology of coupling` is unusually disciplined for this kind of section: you explicitly say phenomenology is “a readout… not evidence independent of it,” which protects against introspective overreach.
- The illusion framing is a real asset. Using garden paths and comparative illusions (`§Grammaticality illusions`) as architecture signatures, not mere nuisance variance, is the right move for psycholinguistics.
- You attempt falsifiability in `§Definiteness thread` with a scoped prediction/falsifier pair. That is exactly the kind of move skeptical philosophers will demand.

Major Concerns
- Mechanistic under-specification is still the biggest weakness. You alternate among “zipper,” “immune system,” “standing wave,” and “spinning top” (`§The stabilizer`, `§Gradient`, `§Dual role`), but there is no explicit process model with representational states, update rules, and decision criteria. A skeptic will say this is still metaphor-led.
- The claim in `§Entrenchment in action` that “the detector isn’t just tracking… the reality is constituted by what gets entrenched” (“all the way down”) risks circularity. If detector output partly constitutes the target, what independent criterion keeps the theory from absorbing all outcomes?
- Acceptability vs grammaticality is not yet fully rigorous. You define acceptability as detector output and grammaticality as coupling (`§The stabilizer`), but then broaden to “appropriateness channels” (`§Grammaticality as a species of appropriateness`) and finally “syntax owns it.” This oscillates between narrow and broad targets without a stable boundary condition.
- Empirical claims are occasionally over-precise or over-strong for current evidence. Examples: “categorically rejected regardless of exposure… across tasks” for LBE, “~3-sigma absence,” and the strong comparative illusion gloss “has no coherent interpretation.” These read as placeholder certainty and invite immediate attack.
- From my field, the first thing rejected will be architecture claims without neural/online commitments. If this is a detector architecture claim, it must predict time course and component structure, not just offline ratings.

Priority Fixes
- Add one explicit computational sketch in `§The stabilizer`: input representation, parser state, prediction/error term, entrenchment prior, and decision threshold producing judgment. One page is enough.
- Separate three levels with strict labels throughout: latent grammatical coupling, online processing dynamics, and metalinguistic judgment policy. Do not let “detector” do all three jobs.
- Replace categorical prose with quantified uncertainty unless you provide direct evidence. Especially in `§Satiation` and LBE discussion.
- Add a compact prediction table mapping chapter claims to measurable signatures (ERP/RT/eye-tracking), including disconfirming patterns.

Decisive Tests
- ERP double dissociation: compare morphosyntactic violations, semantic anomaly, garden paths, and comparative illusions. Core prediction if your account is right: detector misfires should show processing-cost signatures without full morphosyntactic violation signatures; true coupling failures should robustly engage late syntactic repair signals.
- Trial-by-trial adaptation/satiation with EEG + speeded judgments: for exposure-sensitive constraints, expect criterion drift over repetitions (rating shifts) with reduced late reanalysis cost; for putatively categorical constraints (e.g., LBE), no parallel drift.
- Context-indexed entrenchment test (`§Entrenchment in action`): same speakers switched across register/community contexts. If coupling is community-calibrated online, judgments and online processing profiles should shift rapidly and predictably by context, not only by sentence form.
- Pre-register a falsifier stronger than the current one: if entrenchment manipulations move acceptability but leave online structural processing unchanged, your “detector tracks coupling” claim is too strong and must be narrowed.
