# Humour Audit (Codex)

## Chapter 01 — Words That Won't Hold Still
- Humour count: 8 distinct moments.
- Example 1: "But there's something fitting about the image of a linguist awake in the dark, wrestling with a single word."
- Example 2: "The grammar doesn't fail; it proliferates."
- Example 3: "The wave is real. Its edges aren't."
- Assessment: Light but effective dry wit. The chapter is mostly conceptual setup, and the humour level is appropriate: it humanizes the opening without undercutting the stakes.
- Opportunity 1: In the long Rosch/prototype exposition, one extra self-aware aside could help pacing before the denser argument turn.
- Opportunity 2: In the final "impasse" section, a short bathetic line could sharpen the contrast between "crystalline" vs "messy" category pictures.

# Cross-chapter summary

## Funniest chapters
- Chapter 11 (*Lexical categories and their maintenance*) is the clear peak: sustained, varied, and structurally integrated humour.
- Chapter 10 (*Definiteness and Deitality*) and Chapter 8 (*Failure modes*) are close behind for dry comic precision.
- Chapters 14, 15, and 16 are consistently witty while carrying heavy theoretical loads.

## Driest chapters
- Appendix B (*Predictions*) is the driest by far (intentionally ledger-like).
- Appendix A (*Diagnostic methodology*) is also sparse in humour by design.
- Chapter 03 and technical sections of Chapter 05 read relatively dry compared to surrounding chapters.

## Biggest opportunities for improvement
- Chapter 03: add 1-2 extra micro-asides to reduce tonal flatness in long conceptual passages.
- Chapter 05: add light pacing relief in the hyperreal/formal sections.
- Chapter 17: add short voice-forward transitions between gauntlet tables and research agenda blocks.
- Appendix B: add minimal rhetorical warmth while preserving checklist clarity.
