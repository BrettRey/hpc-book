### [R1-T09] Seinfeld
Seinfeld: Adams’s map-not-revelation note is the right anchor, because this is the moment readers either stay with us or feel like they need lab goggles. “With a metric in hand” has that everyday process vibe of finally finding the right setting, but we should cash it out in plain terms immediately. Same object, different local setup, different result: that’s basically everyone’s experience with anything finicky. If we state that explicitly, the basin metaphor feels less mystical and more like normal classification friction.

- `chapters/chapter05.tex:205` — `A metric is a rule for what counts as a small move: change that rule, and some items that looked safely inside a category are suddenly standing at its edge.`
- `chapters/chapter05.tex:213` — `Context effects are this same mechanism in miniature: the item stays put, but local cues tilt the landscape, so the very same step can now cross a boundary.`
