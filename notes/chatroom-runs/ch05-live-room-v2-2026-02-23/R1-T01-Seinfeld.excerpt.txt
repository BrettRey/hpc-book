    11	Thursday, 4 December 2025 was one of the most intellectually exciting days of my life.
    12	
    13	I had conceived of this book~-- or perhaps I'd say the conceptual process had come to a head~-- the previous Saturday, when I created a project folder on my computer called \texttt{HPC book} and sent a synopsis to some friends with the subject line \enquote{Does linguistics need this book?} I've learned to treat the act of naming a folder as the weakest possible form of commitment, but it was enough to start the chain reaction.
    14	
    15	Geoff Pullum said yes. Ryan Nefdt said yes~-- and added something I hadn't thought of:
    16	
    17	\begin{quote}
    18	I personally favour a phase transition approach in which discreteness really is a feature of a system under a particular kind of measurement and context and continuity under a different one (like water moving from liquid to gas takes on different properties). So borrowing more from physics than biology.
    19	\end{quote}
    20	
    21	I woke very early the next morning thinking about phase transitions~-- and about a paper I'd written two months earlier and then set aside. That paper had formalised Sorites tolerance using hyperreal numbers, inspired by work by Toby Ord. It had been \enquote{sitting in a drawer}, waiting for a use. Now it had one.
    22	
    23	By bedtime that evening, I had the first draft of this chapter.
