**Strengths**
- `§The HPC claim` is strongest where it defines the target variable narrowly: grammaticality as coupling of morphosyntax to “structural meaning,” not truth-conditional content. The contrast with `Colorless green ideas...` is well chosen and does real conceptual work.
- `§The stabilizer: a feeling` gives a productive distinction between detector output (acceptability) and underlying state (grammatical coupling). Calling the feeling a “noisy detector” is the right anti-oracular move for skeptical linguists.
- `§The phenomenology of coupling` is commendably self-disciplined in method. You explicitly say phenomenology is a readout, not independent evidence (“mnemonics, not explanatory load-bearing beams”). That restraint increases credibility.
- `§Grammaticality illusions` and `§Empirical signatures` are directionally right: garden paths, comparative illusions, satiation, and individual differences are exactly the kinds of dissociations this framework should exploit.
- The explicit scoped falsifier in the “Definiteness thread” is excellent. Most theoretical chapters avoid such commitments; you make one.

**Major Concerns**
- The chapter still leans too hard on metaphor at crucial inferential points: “zipper,” “immune system,” “standing wave,” “spinning top.” These are vivid, but not yet mechanistic. A skeptic will ask: what are the state variables, update rules, and measurable transitions?
- The acceptability/grammaticality distinction is introduced, then partially collapsed in `§Entrenchment in action` (“tuning the detector” and “shaping what counts as grammatical aren’t separable”). Without explicit timescale separation, this looks like theory-protecting elasticity.
- `§The phenomenology of coupling` maps three registers to sign/object/interpretant, but operational criteria are under-specified. Right now, this can read as retrospective labeling rather than prospective mechanism.
- Several claims are over-precise in ways that look placeholder-like: “~3-sigma absence,” categorical resistance claims for LBE, and strong generalizations from very specific examples. A skeptical reviewer will mark these as precision without calibration.
- The biggest Hacking-style issue is looping. You do gesture to community conditioning and “Homer Simpson” in `§Looking forward`, but classification practices themselves (school correction, style guides, grammar policing, linguists’ own asterisking) are not treated as causal interventions on the target kind. For an interactive kind, that omission is central.
- What a specialist in my field will reject first: the move in `§Grammaticality as a species of appropriateness`. If grammaticality becomes one “channel” among register, accent, stance, etc., you risk dissolving the very specificity you need to claim “syntax owns it.”

**Priority Fixes**
1. Add one explicit mechanistic schema (inputs, priors, parsing cost, repair dynamics, social reinforcement) and map each metaphorical term onto a variable/process.
2. Stabilize the ontology by distinguishing levels and timescales: token judgment, individual grammar, community norm, metalinguistic regime.
3. Expand looping explicitly: how prescriptive classification feeds back into usage and then into judgment distributions.
4. Trim over-precision unless supported by methods details; where evidence is suggestive, label it as such.

**Decisive Tests**
1. **Looping intervention test**: randomly expose matched groups to authoritative “ungrammatical” labeling of a novel but parseable construction; track production + acceptability longitudinally. If classification has no downstream effect, the interactive-kind claim weakens sharply.
2. **Detector/target dissociation test**: train one cohort on rapid exposure (fluency only) and another on explicit form-meaning mapping. If both shift acceptability equally but only mapping shifts production/repair behavior, your detector-vs-coupling distinction is supported.
3. **Three-register dissociation test**: pre-register separate measures for jolt (online disruption), block (repair latency), correction (normative response). Illusions should dissociate jolt from correction in predictable ways.
4. **Appropriateness-channel discriminant test**: model grammaticality, register, and accent judgments as latent factors. If one factor explains all channels, “morphosyntactic grain” is overstated; if separable with controlled interaction, your architecture survives.
