## Chapters Needing Attention

### Ch 3: What we haven't been asking
**Priority: HIGH.** The driest chapter in the book. Heavy philosophical lifting (nominalism, Haspelmath, Croft, Peirce) with almost no relief. Both models agree. The "argue for the drawer" line is the only moment.

*Opportunities:*
- The lexeme obsession section: a self-aware aside about linguists relabelling drawers while claiming not to use them
- The cross-linguistic comparisons (German/Swahili gender, number systems): one "look how weird this gets" moment
- After introducing the nominalist position: a concrete absurdity that makes the abstract stakes visible

### Ch 13: The category zipper
**Priority: HIGH.** Synthesis chapter that feels relentless. Abstract coupling regimes, stabiliser weighting, mediation gradients -- all important, but no relief valve. Both models agree it's the driest in Part III.

*Opportunities:*
- "Academic register" as negative case: a dry observation about recognising it instantly and wishing you didn't
- The polysynthetic discussion: a light touch on the Mohawk "he ruined her dress" example
- The coupling continuum figure: a caption aside

### Ch 5: Discrete from continuous
**Priority: MEDIUM.** The hyperreal math is necessarily heavy, but the sections after the formal material are too earnest. The folder-naming opener is charming; nothing matches it later.

*Opportunities:*
- After the basin visualisation: one "here's where it gets weird" moment
- The multi-category spaces material: a brief deflation

### Ch 6: The Stabilizers
**Priority: MEDIUM.** Foundational theory chapter, so dryness is somewhat appropriate. But 640 lines with 2-3 moments is sparse.

*Opportunities:*
- The particle contrast case: the "Other box on a survey" is already doing work but could be sharpened
- The causal loop diagram: a caption aside about the diagram looking like a conspiracy board

### Ch 17: The gauntlet
**Priority: LOW.** Deliberately sober -- accountability chapter. The Sancho Panza epigraph does what it needs to. Forcing humour here would undermine the gauntlet's authority.

### Ch 2: Essentialism and its discontents
**Priority: LOW.** The dryness is appropriate for methodological critique. The footnote is perfect. "Why essentialism persists" section could use one lighter moment, but the chapter earns its tone.

## What Works (patterns to replicate)

1. **Bathetic deflation** after buildup: "Good luck." / "And they are losing." / "Very has never gone jogging."
2. **Self-aware framing**: folder-naming, noticing the double copula, promissory notes ledger
3. **Concrete absurdity** making abstract stakes visible: trenchcoat metaphor, paper clips, fries-with-a-pronoun
4. **Aphoristic compression** with a bite: "One pooled curve isn't one grammar" / "Hope doesn't moult"
5. **Mock-reassurance**: "Nobody will be harmed except a few traditional labels"

## What to Avoid

- Humour in the formal/mathematical material (Ch 5 hyperreals)
- Humour when engaging directly with other scholars (Rapoport's Rules)
- Forcing levity in the gauntlet (Ch 17) or defeat conditions
- Domestic analogies that trivialise (wedding guests, bad restaurant days)
