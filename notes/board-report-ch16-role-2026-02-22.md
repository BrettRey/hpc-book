## Vote Split

- **A (one final chapter with explicit synthesis + gauntlet sections): 4**
  - Lesser, Croft, Goldberg, Jackendoff
- **C (keep gauntlet-first Chapter 16, add minimal synthesis): 3**
  - McCloskey, Tufte, Godfrey-Smith
- **B (split into two separate chapters): 0**

## Convergences (All or Nearly All Reviewers)

1. **Do not split into two chapters.**  
   No reviewer recommended B.

2. **Chapter 16's gauntlet spine is strong and should remain intact.**  
   Reviewers repeatedly cited defeat conditions, thresholds, and operationalization as the chapter's strongest feature.

3. **Synthesis is the under-served job.**  
   Reviewers across A and C agree Chapter 16 needs a compact synthesis bridge/ledger before or at the start of empirical commitments.

4. **Chapter 15 already provides a strong handoff.**  
   Multiple reviews cite the explicit bridge into Chapter 16 as a reason not to create an additional standalone synthesis chapter.

## Main Disagreement

- **A-camp**: Chapter 16 should *explicitly* be framed as doing both jobs in sequence (synthesis then gauntlet).
- **C-camp**: Current gauntlet-first framing is right; add only a short synthesis module without reframing the chapter's identity.

Operationally, both camps recommend almost the same edit size: a short synthesis section/table inserted before the existing gauntlet machinery.

## Practical Recommendation

Treat this as **A-lite/C+**:

1. Keep Chapter 16 as a single final chapter and keep all existing gauntlet sections.
2. Insert one concise synthesis block near the top (before `The empirical commitments`), e.g.:
   - Title: `Synthesis ledger` or `What the book now commits to`
   - Content: 3-5 cross-book commitments + one compact table:
     - domain/chapter
     - mechanism claim
     - risky prediction
     - defeat signature / measurement lane
3. Keep this block short (roughly 700-1200 words) so gauntlet force is preserved.

This resolves the core concern (under-synthesis) without losing the chapter's current empirical-risk posture.
