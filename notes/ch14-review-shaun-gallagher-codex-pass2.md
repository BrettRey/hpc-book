**Remaining Concerns**

- Pre-reflective disruption is only partially separated from reflective/normative layers. “Reactive resistance” markers (hesitation/repair) can be strategic and audience-shaped, not cleanly phenomenological.
- The normative layer is temporally over-broad (“Turns--years”). Turn-level correction and multi-year norm stabilization are not one mechanism.
- Some claims remain too strong for current evidence: “the progressive…is required” (dialect/register variation), “categorically rejected regardless of exposure” (later softened), and “same immune response” for `*a apple` as for core syntax.
- The chapter still shifts between detector architecture and HPC ontology without a crisp disconfirmation clause for the ontology itself.
