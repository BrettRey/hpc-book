## 1. The Metaphor Problem
Both advisors stumble on "The Stack".

*   **Zimmer**: "Inert layers... classic computer memory." misses the biological dynamism of the argument. Suggests **"Gradient of Constraint"**:
    *   Phoneme constrained by **body** (physics)
    *   Morpheme constrained by **brain** (memory/processing)
    *   Construction constrained by **crowd** (social convention)
*   **Croft**: Risks reifying "levels" that Radical CxG rejects. Morphemes are just minimal constructions. If "The Stack" implies ontological tiers, it fails. Must emphasize it's a **continuum of constructional types** with varying opacity.

## 2. Terminology & Visuals
*   **"Stabiliser-weighting"**: Zimmer calls it "heavy, industrial." Suggests "Mixing Board" or framing mechanisms as active antagonists to entropy ("fighting a war against noise").
*   **Transparent vs Opaque**: Zimmer identifies this as the "lightbulb moment" but suggests framing it as **"Risk Management"**:
    *   Bottom level risk = Physical (mishearing) $\to$ Mechanism: Quantal regions
    *   Higher level risk = Social (misunderstanding) $\to$ Mechanism: Convention/repair

## 3. Empirical Grounding
*   **Phonemes**: Croft warns against using PHOIBLE signatures to claim "universal categories." Distinguish *descriptive categories* (language-specific) from *comparative concepts* (analyst tools). Physics constrains the design space, but doesn't create a universal "Phoneme" kind.
*   **HistWords/Vectors**: Zimmer finds the jump to CS jarring ("natural history to computer science"). Needs a bridge explanations: vectors are "tracing the path of cultural drift."

## 4. The Validation
