**Strengths**
The opening gives you something Morris would call a usable scene: a student, an instructor, a red pen, a cost (`chapters/chapter15.tex:6`). It immediately turns abstraction into stakes. The chapter also treats opponents more fairly than most polemics: you grant formalists a clear puzzle and then reframe it (`chapters/chapter15.tex:21`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:41`). Most importantly for fairness, you explicitly grant nominalists what they got right before arguing where they stop short (`chapters/chapter15.tex:190`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:192`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:194`). That is genuine steelmanning, not ritual concession. The feedback-loop passages in dialect, register, and discourse community keep social claims mechanistic rather than moralizing (`chapters/chapter15.tex:103`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:122`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:133`).

**Major Concerns**
The human beings flatten after page one. The student and instructor become functions ("gatekeeping," "penalizing," "conformity") rather than persons with reasons (`chapters/chapter15.tex:112`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:131`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:147`). That weakens the chapter’s ability to humanize standards and prescriptivism debates. Board guidance explicitly warned against villain framing and asked for point-of-view clarity (`notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:159`, `notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:162`), but the chapter mostly returns to systems language. Nonfiction legibility is also uneven: the opener reads as plausible, but unsourced specifics like "stable across millions" and "10% probability" feel asserted rather than shown (`chapters/chapter15.tex:8`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:41`). Finally, the ending lands on metaphysical confidence ("realism proper") but not a practical handoff (`chapters/chapter15.tex:206`). That misses the board’s request that the close offer a way forward (`notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:165`) and underuses the planned Chapter 16 bridge (`notes/chapter15_planning.md:50`).

**Priority Fixes**
1. Replace one abstract paragraph in each major section with a sourced mini-scene (classroom correction, reviewer comment, hiring-screen register judgment), anchored to a real dataset or attested transcript. Keep one prescriptive actor and one stigmatized speaker onstage in each scene so neither side becomes a cardboard type (`chapters/chapter15.tex:114`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:133`).
2. Restore at least one planned situated-speaker case (the lift example or code-switching) to show people navigating norms, not just categories in equations (`notes/chapter15_planning.md:21`, `notes/chapter15_planning.md:44`).
3. Add a final 6–8 sentence bridge that converts the chapter’s claim into testable next steps, explicitly teeing up Chapter 16’s gauntlet: what to measure, what would count as failure, and what design choice changes tomorrow (`chapters/chapter16.tex:37`, `chapters/chapter16.tex:137`).
