### Strengths
Chapter 15 is strongest when it starts from everyday disciplinary practice and recasts it as a mechanism problem. The opening red-pen scene is concrete, legible, and analytically productive (`chapters/chapter15.tex:6`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:10`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:14`). The mixed-bin argument is also a genuine advance in usable reasoning: you move from pooled variance to conditioned structure, then formalize it as `P(Form | D, R, C)` (`chapters/chapter15.tex:64`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:73`). That gives ordinary social scientists a way to rethink “variation” without abandoning quantitative habits. The chapter also links well to Chapter 14’s community-calibrated detector story (`chapters/chapter14.tex:78`, `chapters/chapter14.tex:241`) and naturally sets up the falsification posture in Chapter 16 (`chapters/chapter16.tex:14`).

### Major Concerns
The main weakness is operational definition. “Dialect,” “register,” and “discourse community” are described persuasively, but not yet in a way that tells a researcher how to code a token, resolve borderline cases, or evaluate coding reliability (`chapters/chapter15.tex:91`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:108`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:127`). Your own planning material flagged exactly this risk and asked for test-ready diagnostics (`notes/chapter15_planning.md:29`, `notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:154`).  

Second, testability is asserted more than scaffolded. Claims like “the question is ill-posed” and “ontologically distinct” raise the theoretical bar (`chapters/chapter15.tex:41`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:118`), but the chapter does not yet supply a minimum viable empirical protocol for teams with small corpora, limited tagging labor, and no advanced modeling support. Chapter 16 already shows the needed style: observables, defeat thresholds, and explicit comparison rules (`chapters/chapter16.tex:99`, `chapters/chapter16.tex:123`).  

Third, a few turns of phrase risk unnecessary disciplinary resistance. “Descriptions, not mechanisms” and “they were wrong” may be defensible, but can read as tribal dismissal (`chapters/chapter15.tex:95`, `chapters/chapter15.tex:194`). The Becker note in your board file is right: raise standards without attacking incumbents (`notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:152`).

### Priority Fixes
Add one compact subsection that operationalizes D/R/C as research variables: observable proxies, tagging rules, adjudication procedure, and a minimal reliability criterion, tied directly to the existing formula (`chapters/chapter15.tex:73`). Then add one ordinary-researcher test design: modest corpus, simple model (or contingency-table fallback), two predicted signatures, and one falsifier aligned with Chapter 16’s gauntlet logic (`chapters/chapter16.tex:112`, `chapters/chapter16.tex:124`). Finally, revise high-friction framing to cumulative language (“extends variable-rules findings by modeling conditioning structure”) and include one sentence acknowledging practical field constraints, as requested in the planning board notes (`notes/ch15/board-ch15-plan-notes.md:157`).
