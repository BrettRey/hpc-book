\chapter{The category zipper}\ixsq{zipper}
\label{ch:the-category-zipper}

\epigraph{If you have two complementary strands of DNA, they zip up. That's what they do.}{~-- Sri Kosuri, quoted in \textit{Harvard Gazette} (2019)}

Part~III examined four core grammatical categories from the inside~-- countability\ixsq{countability}, definiteness, lexical categories, gender~-- each with its own cluster, its own stabilizers, its own pattern of boundary behaviour; then Chapter~\ref{ch:sign-anaphor} added a modality stress test in sign language. This chapter steps back to map the shared architecture: not isolated case studies in a row, but coordinated points on a single mechanism-defined gradient.

Section~\ref{sec:8:coupling} introduced a coupling continuum: hard-coupled HPCs, where form directly realizes contrastive value\ixsq{value}; loosely coupled HPCs, where form and meaning drift independently; composite HPCs, where constructions bind them together conventionally. That continuum turns out to illuminate not just the grammatical categories of Part~III but the full range of linguistic organization, from phoneme\ixsq{phoneme} to construction (Figure~\ref{fig:14:coupling-spectrum}).

The organizing question is constant across this range. At some levels, form largely determines the object\ixsq{object} and the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} mostly follows. At others, the object\ixsq{object} has to be inferred and the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} does active mediating work. At others still, the unit exists only because a conventional inferential habit\ixsq{habit} makes it exist. What shifts from level to level is where the coupling bears its load and which stabilizers keep it in place.

The chapter proceeds by controlled ascent. We start at the hard end, where the system is so tightly coupled that even its mistakes are well-behaved~-- wrong in the expected way. Then we move to words, where form--object\ixsq{object} pairing is arbitrary but retrieval is stable. Then to grammatical categories, where semantic and formal clusters negotiate alignment\ixsq{alignment!convergence effects}. Then to constructions, where cue bundles produce a gestalt and the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} constitutes the unit.

\section{Hard coupling: phonemes}
\ixsq{hard coupling}
\label{sec:14:phonemes}
%~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--

The phoneme\ixsq{phoneme} tier is where hard coupling\ixsq{hard coupling} is most visible. The three terms of the triadic structure (§\ref{sec:7:what-projection-is}) are distinct and independently measurable:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Form}: acoustic signal~-- formant distributions, voice-onset time, spectral shape.
    \item \textbf{Object\ixsq{object}}: contrastive identity~-- its value\ixsq{value}~-- /\ipa{k}/ as not-/\ipa{g}/, not-/\ipa{t}/, not-/\ipa{p}/.
    \item \textbf{Interpretant\ixsq{interpretant}}: lexical access and downstream processing~-- hearing /\ipa{k}/ triggers retrieval of \mention{cat}\ixlq{cat}, not \mention{gat}\ixlq{gat}; inferences follow.
\end{itemize}

The coupling is transparent: form largely determines the object\ixsq{object}, which in turn determines the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant}, with minimal slippage. Errors predominantly stay inside the system~-- one phoneme\ixsq{phoneme} for another~-- because the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} follows the object\ixsq{object} without further computation.

What maintains this tight coupling? The stabilizers are the causal-maintenance mechanisms. Here we find four stabilizer\ixsq{stabilizer!types of} types. First, quantal regions: \ixnq{Stevens, Kenneth N.}\textcite{stevens1989} showed that the articulatory-to-acoustic mapping is non-linear, with certain configurations producing stable outputs across a range of variation~-- natural \enquote{parking spots} that constrain what phoneme\ixsq{phoneme} contrasts are \emph{possible}. Second, dispersion pressure: \ixnq{Lindblom, Bjorn@Lindblom, Björn}\textcite{lindblom1990} argued that vowel inventories disperse in acoustic space to maximize discriminability, shaping which of the possible phonemes a language \emph{selects}. Third, perceptual tuning acting as a stabilization mechanism: \ixnq{Kuhl, Patricia K.}\ixnq{Williams, Karen A.}\ixnq{Lacerda, Francisco}\ixnq{Stevens, Kenneth N.}\ixnq{Lindblom, Bjorn@Lindblom, Björn}\textcite{kuhl1992} demonstrated that infant perception is warped by early exposure, with category boundaries becoming phonetic attractors that tune the individual speaker to the community standard. Fourth, community norms: social transmission across generations stabilizes inventories through the mechanisms that cultural-tool accounts emphasize \ixnq{Ekstrom, Axel G.@Ekström, Axel G.}\ixnq{Tennie, Claudio}\ixnq{Moran, Steven}\ixnq{Everett, Caleb}\parencite{ekstrom2025}.

Evidence comes from PHOIBLE 2.0 \ixnq{Moran, Steven}\ixnq{McCloy, Daniel}\parencite{moran2019phoible}, a database capturing 3,020 inventories across 2,186 distinct languages. Two patterns are consistent with the diagnostics. Plotting kernel-density ridgelines of total inventory sizes by family reveals a \emph{stability band}: medians often cluster between 20 and 50 segments across unrelated families, with thin tails beyond. This pattern is compatible with cross-family regularity enabling inventory-level projection. Meanwhile, the vowel /y/~-- a front rounded vowel requiring precise articulatory coordination~-- shows a \emph{scaling curve}: a logistic model predicting /y/-presence from the total vowel count of a language's inventory (cross-validated via grouped splits to block family-level confounding) discriminates effectively between inventories that have the sound and those that don't (in our full-stack analysis, Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic curve, ROC-AUC $\approx 0.70$, well above the 0.50 baseline of chance guessing; \ixnq{Reynolds, Brett}\citealt{reynolds2025hpcbook}).\footnote{The Python scripts for the PHOIBLE, HistWords, and UD corpus analyses are available at \url{https://github.com/BrettRey/hpc-book/tree/main/code}.} This predictive power is consistent with the claim that biological constraints of articulation shape the inventory even as individual sounds shift. Marked segments lacking quantal robustness appear mainly in larger systems where there's acoustic room.

The \mention{pin}\ixlq{pin}/\mention{pen}\ixlq{pen} merger provides a stress test. In much of the American South and parts of the Midland, /\ipa{ɪ}/ and /\ipa{ɛ}/ have merged before nasal consonants \ixnq{Labov, William}\ixnq{Ash, Sharon}\ixnq{Boberg, Charles}\parencite{labov2006}. Speakers produce the same vowel in \mention{pin}\ixlq{pin} and \mention{pen}\ixlq{pen}; many speakers can't reliably distinguish the two words by ear. If you grew up with the merger, you can treat this paragraph as a live demonstration: it really is describing one vowel twice. The conditioning environment is revealing: before nasals, the vowels are subject to nasalization, which smears the formant cues. In exactly the environment where acoustic cues are least reliable, the contrast\ixsq{contrast} collapses. This is form--object\ixsq{object} coupling failing in a predictable way. The interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} follows~-- speakers can't distinguish the words~-- because two forms now map to the same object\ixsq{object}. The triadic structure makes the failure legible.

Hard coupling\ixsq{hard coupling} is what happens when the vulnerable link is shallow: small changes in form change the object\ixsq{object}, and the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} follows with little mediation. When the coupling does fail (as in the \mention{pin}\ixlq{pin}/\mention{pen}\ixlq{pen} merger), the failure is local and predictable, because the system is built to keep contrasts audible.

Move one level up and the link that stays vulnerable is still form--object\ixsq{object}, but the reason changes. The next tier is driven not by acoustic necessity, but by arbitrary convention stabilized in memory.

\section{Opaque coupling: words}
\label{sec:14:words}
%~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--

One level up, the coupling loosens. At the phoneme\ixsq{phoneme} level, form realizes contrast\ixsq{contrast}. At the word level, form merely points. The form \mention{went}\ixlq{went} is past by convention, not by decomposable structure~-- the connection is stored whole, not derived. This is \term{opaque coupling}. The system stores it like a password: you either know it or you don't.

The triadic structure at the word level:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Form}: orthographic/phonological shape~-- \mention{went}\ixlq{went}, \mention{go}\ixlq{go}, \mention{dog}\ixlq{dog}.
    \item \textbf{Object\ixsq{object}}: conventional pairing~-- pastness, motion, canine-kind. The connection is arbitrary; you can't read the object\ixsq{object} off the form.
    \item \textbf{Interpretant\ixsq{interpretant}}: the inferences the word enables~-- temporal location, argument-structure expectations, encyclopedic knowledge activation.
\end{itemize}

The coupling is opaque at form--object\ixsq{object} (brute memory) but stable at object\ixsq{object}--interpretant (once you've accessed the object\ixsq{object}, the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} follows). Errors at this level are characteristically retrieval failures, not composition failures.

\ixnq{Miller, J. T. M.}\textcite{miller2021} develops an HPC\ixsq{homeostatic property cluster!vs.\ essentialism} stance at the level of particular lexemes~-- \mention{dog}\ixlq{dog}, \mention{run}\ixlq{run}, \mention{egregious}\ixlq{egregious}~-- rejecting essence-based individuation in favour of mechanism-indexed clusters that are historically delimited and population-relative. The lexeme \mention{dog}\ixlq{dog} maintains its identity not through a platonic essence but because spelling conventions, pronunciation norms, semantic associations, and syntactic patterns travel together, stabilized by orthographic standardization\ixsq{standardization} (educational institutions, publishing practices), frequency entrenchment (repetition in memory automating retrieval; \ixnq{Bybee, Joan}\citealt{bybee2001}), editorial norms (copy-editing workflows flagging nonstandard usage), and register licensing (genre conventions sanctioning certain words in certain contexts).

The question is whether words can change semantically and still remain HPC\ixsq{homeostatic property cluster!membership} kinds. The HistWords COHA embeddings \ixnq{Hamilton, William L.}\ixnq{Leskovec, Jure}\ixnq{Jurafsky, Dan}\parencite{hamilton2016} provide decade-binned distributional representations for English words over the 20th century. (Embeddings capture distributional neighbours, not meaning directly, but neighbourhood stability is a useful proxy for cluster coherence.) High-drift adjectives (top decile of average cosine displacement, with documented drift terms like \mention{nice}\ixlq{nice}, \mention{sick}\ixlq{sick}, \mention{gay}\ixlq{gay}, \mention{awful}\ixlq{awful} forced in) are compared to frequency-matched controls with minimal drift. Some drift adjectives retain organized neighbourhoods even as their centres move: for \mention{nice}\ixlq{nice}, nearest neighbours shift from \{pretty, lovely, pleasant\} in the 1900s to \{cute, wonderful, really\} by the 2000s; for \mention{sick}\ixlq{sick}, from \{ill, tired, hungry\} to \{hurt, drunk, upset\}. A nearest-centroid prototype classifier\ixsq{classifier} trained on early decades (1900--1940) and tested on later decades (1950--2000) recovers semantic class assignments (over 33 WordNet supersense domains) with a balanced accuracy metric (in our analysis, a macro-averaged F1 score, macro-F1, of 0.84, heavily outperforming the 0.03 class-stratified random-guessing baseline; \ixnq{Reynolds, Brett}\citealt{reynolds2025hpcbook}). This classifier performance suggests that historical drift often creates measurable, cohesive trajectories rather than random isotopic decay. Combined with a cohesion cutoff, only a subset of high-drift adjectives satisfies both criteria. The framework tells us to withhold kindhood for those lexeme--time slices rather than forcing a positive verdict. For the subset that passes, past usage fixes expectations that carry forward~-- as the HPC\ixsq{homeostatic property cluster!membership} picture predicts.

The \mention{go}\ixlq{go}--\mention{went}\ixlq{went} pattern provides a stress test. If form--object\ixsq{object} pairings are maintained by frequency and analogy\ixsq{analogy}, high-frequency irregulars should resist regularization; low-frequency irregulars should be vulnerable. \ixnq{Bybee, Joan}\textcite{bybee2001} documents exactly this. High-frequency irregulars (\mention{go}\ixlq{go}--\mention{went}\ixlq{went}, \mention{have}\ixlq{have}--\mention{had}\ixlq{had}) show no regularization pressure. Mid-frequency irregulars (\mention{weave}\ixlq{weave}--\mention{wove}\ixlq{wove}) show variable forms. Low-frequency irregulars (\mention{cleave}\ixlq{cleave}--\mention{clove}\ixlq{clove}) have largely regularized. Frequency protects the arbitrary pairing; analogy\ixsq{analogy} regularizes the unprotected. The two stabilizers interact in predictable ways.

Opaque coupling is still coupling: once the object\ixsq{object} is retrieved, the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} is stable. What's fragile is the arbitrariness itself, and the stabilizers that protect it are the ones that protect conventions generally: frequency, analogy\ixsq{analogy}, and norms.

The next tier raises a different problem. In grammatical categories, form--object\ixsq{object} arbitrariness is no longer the main source of vulnerability. Instead, there are \emph{two} objects in play~-- semantic and formal clusters~-- and the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} has to keep them aligned.

\section{Loose coupling: the grammatical categories}
\ixsq{loose coupling}
\label{sec:14:grammatical-categories}
%~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--

Between the hard coupling\ixsq{hard coupling} of phonemes and the composite coupling\ixsq{composite coupling} of constructions sits the territory Part~III has been exploring. What Part~III establishes is that \enquote{loose coupling\ixsq{loose coupling}} isn't structural autonomy; it's flexibility of mapping. The semantic and formal poles don't exist in ontological isolation. Instead, they form a two-cluster architecture coupled by inference\ixsq{inference}, maintained by overlapping mechanisms, and projectible~-- knowing the cluster tells you something about cases you haven't seen. But they can develop independently. Homesigners with no access to an established sign language can assemble abstract agent/patient role structure from structured experience alone \citep{canudas-grabolosa2026linguisticModel}; the semantic pole of the coupling is in place before any formal pole grammaticalizes it.

Form and meaning are usually partners here, but not handcuffed.

The four core category studies~-- together with the sign-language stress test~-- illustrate the spectrum of this flexibility. Rather than a monolithic grammar module, we see a topography of coupling tightness, with different categories occupying different positions (Table~\ref{tab:14:matrix}).

Loose coupling\ixsq{loose coupling} isn't looseness of structure; it's looseness of \emph{mapping}. The clusters are real and maintained, but the alignment\ixsq{alignment!convergence effects} is negotiated and field\ixsq{field}-relative, which is why the same lexical space can support multiple honest carvings. When several carvings are defensible, that doesn't mean the data are fuzzy~-- it means the same material serves multiple purposes, and different analytical perspectives legitimately foreground different ones.

If this were the whole story, though, the framework would be too generous. Before moving to constructions, it needs to show it can refuse kindhood when it should.

\section{Negative cases: when the framework says no}
\label{sec:14:negative}
%~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--

The HPC\ixsq{homeostatic property cluster!negative cases} framework would be toothless if it granted kindhood to every stable pattern. The negative cases are where the diagnostics do their most useful work, and there are three instructive ones.

\emph{Academic register\ixsq{register}} has a recognizable flavour: passive constructions, nominalizations, hedges like \mention{it has been argued}. The features cluster; experienced readers identify academic prose instantly~-- often before the main verb shows up. But this clustering isn't an HPC category\ixsq{category!vs.\ class}; it's a \term{conditioning effect} (see Chapter~\ref{ch:social-stabilization}). The \enquote{Academic} cluster is just the output of situational reweighting. Put the same researcher in front of a grant panel, a blog audience, and a conference poster, and the passives evaporate, the hedges reweight. The cluster doesn't resist perturbation because it's not a stable entity~-- it dissolves when the situation changes. It fails the diagnostic because it describes a temporary state of the system, not a stable component.

\emph{Indo-European} is a language family defined by historical descent. The cluster properties~-- cognate vocabulary, shared sound correspondences, similar grammatical patterns~-- are real. But ask what would push English\ixgq{English} \emph{back toward} Proto-Indo-European\ixgq{Proto-Indo-European}. Nothing. Languages do not have a reverse gear. There's no homeostatic pressure maintaining Indo-European-ness. English\ixgq{English} drifts away from PIE\ixgq{Proto-Indo-European} continuously; contact with non-IE languages accelerates the drift. Historical kinds are defined by causal continuity with an origin; homeostatic kinds are defined by stabilizers that maintain covariance. Confusing them invites explanatory error.

\emph{Polysynthetic languages} are characterized by high morpheme-to-word ratios, incorporating structures, and complex verb templates. Mohawk\ixgq{Mohawk}, Chukchi, and Ainu are standard examples. But the same stabilizers don't maintain polysynthesis across families. Mohawk\ixgq{Mohawk} (Iroquoian) has noun incorporation for discourse-pragmatic reasons~-- incorporated nouns are non-referential \ixnq{Mithun, Marianne}\parencite{mithun1984}. A single Mohawk\ixgq{Mohawk} word like \mention{Washakotya'tawitsherahetkvhta'se} encodes what English\ixgq{English} requires a full clause for: \enquote{he made the thing that one puts on one's body ugly for her}~-- i.e., \enquote{he ruined her dress} \ixnq{Baker, Mark C.}\parencite[40]{baker1996}. Chukchi (Chukotko-Kamchatkan) has incorporation with different constraints and different discourse functions. The surface similarity~-- complex words~-- masks heterogeneous routes. There are likely local, genealogically bounded homeostatic categories that yield polysynthetic-looking outputs, but polysynthesis as a global category\ixsq{category!cross-linguistic} is a \emph{multiply realizable} property, like \enquote{having wheels,} not a cross-family HPC category\ixsq{category!cross-linguistic}. \enquote{Polysynthetic} is a typological region label, useful for description but not for explanation: it doesn't name a single, shared, mechanism-maintained category.

The three cases fail for different reasons~-- conditioning effect rather than stable kind (academic register), historical rather than homeostatic maintenance (Indo-European), heterogeneous routes to surface similarity (polysynthetic). The value of explicit diagnostics is that they distinguish these failure modes: you can say \emph{why} something doesn't qualify, not just that it doesn't.

\subsection{The null case}
\label{sec:14:null-case}
If the framework is correct, we should also find a \emph{null case}: a robust semantic pole that simply never develops a morphosyntactic coupling in a given language. Consider grammatical definiteness in Russian\ixgq{Russian}. The semantic concepts of identifiability\ixsq{identifiability} and uniqueness\ixsq{uniqueness} exist~-- Russian\ixgq{Russian} speakers track discourse referents perfectly well~-- but Russian\ixgq{Russian} doesn't conventionalize definiteness\ixsq{definiteness} into an obligatory determiner slot the way English\ixgq{English} does (even though other Slavic\ixgq{Slavic languages} languages, like Bulgarian\ixgq{Bulgarian} and Macedonian, do). Russian\ixgq{Russian} speakers use demonstratives, word order, and bare nouns to manage information structure\ixsq{information structure}, but these are locally motivated strategies; what's absent is any obligatory morphosyntactic commitment in the NP. When coupling fails to develop, the communicative load gets absorbed by lexical or constructional alternatives~-- which means the coupling continuum extends all the way to zero.

These failures matter because they aren't all the same failure. A conditioning effect can be real without being a kind\ixsq{kind!vs.\ class}; a historical lineage can be real without being homeostatic; a typological label can be useful without naming a shared stabilizer\ixsq{stabilizer!as mechanism role}. What the negative cases show is that the diagnostics do substantive work, not just that the framework is flexible enough to accommodate anything.

Now we can move to the tier where the unit is constituted by a conventional inferential habit\ixsq{habit}: composite coupling\ixsq{composite coupling} in constructions.

\section{Composite coupling: constructions}
\ixsq{composite coupling}
\label{sec:14:constructions}
%~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--

Above the level of individual categories, constructions re-bind what loose coupling\ixsq{loose coupling} lets drift. Conventionalized pairings of form and meaning~-- the ditransitive, the \mention{let alone} construction, the way-construction~-- rely on multiple converging cues that speakers recognize as a gestalt. The form--object\ixsq{object} coupling is \emph{composite}: built up from components, maintained holistically by use, transmitted through interaction.

The triadic structure at the construction\ixsq{construction} level:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Form}: the formal template~-- word order, morphological marking, prosodic contour.
    \item \textbf{Object\ixsq{object}}: the semantic/pragmatic template~-- what the construction\ixsq{construction} \emph{means} as a conventional pairing.
    \item \textbf{Interpretant\ixsq{interpretant}}: the discourse expectations it sets up~-- what the hearer is licensed to infer, what follow-up is appropriate.
\end{itemize}

The object\ixsq{object} here is a conventional inference\ixsq{inference} package, often polysemous but constrained; the diagnostic is whether cue bundles stabilize those constraints across contexts.

The coupling is composite: form--object\ixsq{object} links are conventional but compositional; interpretants must be computed from assembled pieces. When the composition fails~-- local form--object\ixsq{object} links are fine, but the global interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} doesn't cohere~-- the result is a garden-path or a misparse.

Three stabilizer\ixsq{stabilizer!types of} types maintain constructions. Frequency and entrenchment\ixsq{entrenchment!frequency effects} work on millisecond-to-week timescales: each token use strengthens memory traces, increasing production probability, generating more tokens in a self-reinforcing loop \ixnq{Bybee, Joan}\parencite{bybee2001}. Cue redundancy provides robustness: multiple formal features converge on the same interpretation. Consider the \mention{or even}\ixlq{even} construction (\enquote{He didn't read the article, or even the abstract.}). The canonical cues include negative polarity in the anchor, the coordinator \mention{or}, the scalar particle \mention{even}\ixlq{even}, and structural parallelism. If parallelism diverges while grammaticality remains intact (\enquote{He didn't read the article, or even skimming the abstract was skipped}), the scalar inference\ixsq{inference} requires costly repair. The bundle of cues maintains the cognitive gestalt. Normative pressure operates on year-to-decade timescales: editorial practices and style guides reinforce the canonical pattern, especially in formal registers.

If the construction\ixsq{construction} tier is to do more than demonstrate feasibility, the diagnostics have to survive contact with heterogeneous constructions. This tier is demonstrated in English because the modelling pipeline is implemented there; the typological prediction is that form--value gestalts across languages will exhibit comparable stabilization signatures, and the disconfirmer would be a system where robust functional complexes show no measurable cue redundancy or normative stabilization. The confirmatory analysis uses a battery of ten constructions spanning four cue regimes, tested across UD English corpora (GUM, EWT, GUMReddit).\footnote{For transparency, Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics} reports corpus sizes, positive-instance counts per construction per corpus, prevalence baselines for PR-AUC, and the feature inventory for each cue regime. The evaluation is strict transfer: models are trained on GUM (with a held-out development split for thresholding) and tested without retuning on EWT; GUMReddit serves as a register stress test.}
Eight are treated as positive candidates; two are included as designed brakes cases~-- pooled resultatives (predicted \enquote{too fat}) and \mention{X much?} (predicted register-local)~-- to act as theoretical controls (so the framework can't just coast downhill into yes). These are cases where constructionhood is either subtype-fragmented or register-parasitic, so HPC should fail, forcing the framework to say no when it should.\footnote{The negative-control criteria are pre-registered as follows: a candidate fails if (i) transfer performance collapses to near-baseline in at least one out-of-domain corpus, and (ii) ablation produces no stable cue-family signature (i.e., no cue family yields a consistent marginal drop across corpora).}
For the \mention{or even} construction, a classifier trained on GUM and tested on EWT identifies true usages amidst noise with strong transfer performance in this setup (in our extended analysis, an Area Under the Precision--Recall Curve, PR-AUC, of 0.886 for the full bundle; \ixnq{Reynolds, Brett}\citealt{reynolds2025hpcbook});\footnote{Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics} reports $n$ for positives and negatives in both corpora, the prevalence baseline PR-AUC, and bootstrap intervals over documents. Thresholds are chosen on the GUM development split and fixed for EWT.}
dropping parallelism degrades the model's ability to find the construction (PR-AUC drops to 0.612).\footnote{\enquote{Parallelism} is operationalized as the subset of features encoding scalar alignment across conjuncts/alternatives (e.g., syntactic symmetry, matched category sequences, and comparative markers). The appendix reports the full ablation table and run-to-run variance so the parallelism drop can be distinguished from model noise.}
Ablation signatures show parallelism as a dominant stabilizer\ixsq{stabilizer!types of} for scalar-additive constructions.\footnote{Here \emph{dominant} means: among cue-family ablations, parallelism yields the largest and most consistent marginal loss in PR-AUC across corpora for scalar-additive targets, while other cue families produce smaller, less stable losses.}
This outcome was genuinely surprising: we initially expected pooled resultatives to behave like well-behaved, stable constructions given their prominence in the theoretical literature. Instead, the pooled resultative shows weak cross-corpus transfer and washed-out ablation signatures~-- failing to transfer robustly across domains. It was only when viewed as a fragmented category lacking cohesive stabilization that the failure made sense, consistent with the \enquote{too fat} diagnosis.\footnote{Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics} reports transfer PR-AUC relative to baseline and shows that no cue-family ablation yields a reproducible large drop, consistent with heterogeneous subtypes. Where subtype-stratification is feasible, the appendix reports sharper cue signatures within subtypes.}

The implication is deliberately narrow: the construction\ixsq{construction} tier doesn't license blanket HPC\ixsq{homeostatic property cluster!negative cases} claims. Each candidate has to pass the diagnostics on its own merits.

Composite coupling\ixsq{composite coupling} is where the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} does the most visible work. The unit isn't merely retrieved; it must be inferred as a coherent gestalt. The stabilizers that matter are therefore the ones that protect that composition: cue redundancy, and~-- for orthography-dependent or high-prestige variants~-- norm enforcement and genre licensing.

The remaining question isn't whether the tiers differ, but how the differences line up. That's what the stabilizer\ixsq{stabilizer!types of}-weighting map is for. The map includes a discourse tier~-- topic, focus, information structure~-- for architectural completeness, but developing that case requires evidence this book hasn't assembled. Section~\ref{sec:17:research-agenda} flags it as an open front.

The stabilizers at each tier don't reduce to a single cause. At the phoneme\ixsq{phoneme} tier, biophysical constraints carve the design space, developmental learning binds cues, and sociocultural norms transmit inventories. At the word tier, frequency entrenches forms, editorial standards enforce conventions, and usage communities police extensions. At the construction tier, cue redundancy protects against noise, normative pressure corrects deviations, and genre licensing regulates distribution. The balance shifts~-- articulatory constraints weigh heavily for phonemes, frequency and norms for words, cue redundancy and editorial pressure for constructions~-- but at every tier, body, cognition, and society all contribute.

Table~\ref{tab:14:stabilizer-map} maps vulnerable links to stabilizer\ixsq{stabilizer!types of} families. It also points to a deeper regularity: as we move up the stack, what changes isn't merely \emph{which} stabilizers dominate, but \emph{how much mediation is required} for the system to do its work. That's what the next section makes explicit.

\section{The mediation gradient}
\label{sec:14:mediation}
%~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--

The coupling continuum has a Peircean interpretation. In the triadic structure of sign, object\ixsq{object}, and interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} (§\ref{sec:7:what-projection-is}), the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant}'s role shifts as we move along the zipper\ixsq{zipper}. Thirdness is present across the whole continuum; what changes is how constitutive, negotiable, and conventionally enforced the interpretant's contribution is.

At the phoneme\ixsq{phoneme} level, the interpretant is nearly automatic. Form determines object\ixsq{object} (contrastive identity); object\ixsq{object} determines interpretant (lexical access). Uptake can look near-indexical~-- fast, low-effort, strongly constrained~-- but the relation is still triadic semiosis rather than bare covariation. The relation holds because of tight constraint-based physical and perceptual mappings, not because the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} \emph{constitutes} it.

At the word level, the interpretant does more. The form--object\ixsq{object} pairing is symbolic and conventional~-- brute memory, not acoustic necessity~-- and the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} must be learned, not merely perceived. But once activated, it follows reliably. The habit\ixsq{habit} is stored, not computed.

At the grammatical-category level, mediation becomes central. The interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} doesn't just follow from the form--object\ixsq{object} pairing; it \emph{mediates} between a semantic construal and a morphosyntactic form, coupling them through bidirectional inference\ixsq{bidirectional inference}. This is the territory of would-bes: the category\ixsq{category!as HPC kind} generates inductive habits that extend to unencountered instances. The interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} is what the maintenance buys.

At the construction\ixsq{construction} level, we have habit\ixsq{habit}-constituted symbols: the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} constitutes the unit. Without the conventionalized inferential habit\ixsq{habit}, the form--value pairing doesn't exist as a construction\ixsq{construction}~-- it's just a sequence of words.

The gradient isn't a ladder of complexity but a map of where the would-be\ixsq{would-be} bears the load. Semiosis stays triadic all the way through. At the hard end, stable form--object\ixsq{object} covariation carries more of the burden and mediation is light. At the composite end, conventional inferential mediation carries most of it. The grammatical categories of Part~III sit in the middle~-- the zone where the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} mediates actively, where would-bes are conditional on field\ixsq{field} and purpose, and where the coupling is loose enough that different analytical perspectives can carve differently (§\ref{sec:7:field-relative}). Grammatical categories generate the most disagreement precisely because they inhabit this middle zone: mediation is substantial enough to sustain real projectibility\ixsq{projectibility!field-relative}, and the resulting categories reward field\ixsq{field}-relative decomposition rather than single-answer classification.

\section{Predictions and disconfirmers}
\label{sec:14:predictions}
%~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--

The diagnostics generate falsifiable predictions beyond the case studies. In effect: kick a stabilizer and the wobble should show up where the framework says it should. Weakening a stabilizer\ixsq{stabilizer!perturbation sensitivity} should reduce cluster covariance before norms re-stabilize; for constructions, downsampling training data to 25\% should degrade PR-AUC substantially if the bundle depends critically on frequency. Just as /y/ appears preferentially in larger vowel inventories, rare constructional variants should concentrate in corpora with larger constructicon repertoires. If a pooled category like \enquote{resultative} is genuinely \enquote{too fat}, stratifying into subtypes should restore projectibility\ixsq{projectibility!induction from} for well-maintained local kinds. These tests operationalize the core claim: linguistic categories qualify as maintained categories when they project via identifiable mechanisms, and qualify as strict HPCs when they additionally show homeostatic attractor-recovery dynamics.

\section{The architecture of maintenance}
\label{sec:14:forward}
%~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--  ~--

The same framework~-- property clusters, stabilizing mechanisms, projectibility\ixsq{projectibility}~-- applies from the phoneme to the construction. What changes across levels isn't the logic but the coupling: how tightly form and meaning bind, where the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} bears the load, and which stabilizers dominate.

The diagnostic test stays the same; only the material changes by level.

That's the scale-invariant claim, and it's worth being precise about what's invariant. The \emph{diagnostic criteria} remain constant across levels, while the \emph{realizer families} and the \emph{explanatory depth} of the stabilizers vary radically. The HPC\ixsq{homeostatic property cluster!two-diagnostic test} diagnostics don't privilege a level. The ontological character of the stabilizers shifts~-- from biophysics for phonemes, to cognitive frequency for words, to social norms for constructions~-- but phonemes, words, grammatical categories, and constructions all either pass or fail the same tests. The tests themselves are constant in form, though their operationalization is purpose-relative, which is why negative controls and cross-corpus transfer aren't optional.

But one location has special status. Throughout Part~III~-- countability, definiteness, lexical categories, gender~-- the categories clustered at the morphosyntactic level. That's where form--object\ixsq{object}--interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} coupling is both \emph{tight} and \emph{obligatory}. You can speak without using \mention{let alone}. In English, finite clauses force tense; many NP environments force number; determiner--head packaging is unusually tight even when determiners aren't obligatory.

Morphosyntax is the zone of maximum systematicity: the region of grammar where a \emph{semantic} interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} is enforced, unlike phonemes where only a discriminatory interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} is obligatory. It's the part of the system where the grammar stops being politely optional and starts being bossy.

Composite coupling\ixsq{composite coupling} makes it natural to ask whether some parts of the system behave as if they were \emph{packaged} for transmission\ixsq{transmission}: if constructions are maintained by bundles of cues, are there domains where the bundle is so tight that separation is systematically resisted? One place to look is inside the nominal domain.

We can measure this tightness. \ixnq{Reynolds, Brett}\textcite{reynolds2026lbe} quantifies the \term{packaging tightness} of the determiner--head relationship in English~-- the link that the Left Branch Gap (§\ref{sec:8:negative}) fails to break. Using a dependency-locality metric, he finds that determiners and their heads exhibit a packaging score of $k=4$ (extremely tight).\footnote{To make $k$ interpretable, the appendix defines the metric (units, normalization, and typical range) and reports comparison scores for other head--dependent relations (e.g., adjective--head, auxiliary--verb), along with corpus, sample size, and genre sensitivity.}
The system treats $D+N$ not as neighbours but as a rigid transmission\ixsq{transmission} unit. This is what obligatory interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} generation looks like in the data: a coupling so strong that the elements refuse to be separated.

Chapter~\ref{ch:grammaticality-itself} asks what happens when we take this observation seriously. If grammaticality\ixsq{grammaticality} is what emerges when form--object\ixsq{object}--interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} coupling is obligatory, compositional, and learnable, then grammaticality\ixsq{grammaticality} itself is a kind\ixsq{kind!linguistic kinds as}~-- and the obligatoriness matters. At this level, the interpretant\ixsq{interpretant} isn't optional the way it can be for constructions; the system forces it.

Kosuri's remark about DNA is literal, but the parallel holds for language too. Complementary strands zip up because the chemistry makes that configuration the stable one. In language, form and meaning couple wherever the mechanisms make coupling cheap and decoupling costly. The question for the next chapter is what it means to treat that obligatory coupling~-- the feeling that certain pairings \emph{must} hold~-- as itself a maintained kind.
